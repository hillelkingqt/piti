[2025-07-01 10:53:08] [ID: true_120363316946072293@g.us_3FB96B42EAED09555B88_200644128440439@lid] ×¤×™×ª×™ (media content): This document is a summary of topics covered in an Infinitesimal Calculus 2 course, authored by Hillel Ben Hanokh and based on lectures by Shahar Shenbo. It covers a wide range of subjects typically found in a second-semester or second-year calculus course.

The summary is structured into seven main sections:

1.  **Indefinite Integral:** Defines the indefinite integral and antiderivative, discusses integration methods (immediate integrals, integration by parts, substitution), introduces the differential, and covers integration of rational functions and special substitutions.
2.  **Definite Integral:** Defines the Riemann integral, explains Darboux sums and integrability conditions, presents the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (both parts), outlines properties and methods for definite integrals (linearity, additivity, monotonicity, inequality, mean value theorem, integration by parts, substitution).
3.  **Applications of the Definite Integral:** Provides formulas and methods for calculating areas (under curves, between curves), volumes of solids of revolution (disk/washer and shell methods), arc length, and surface area of revolution.
4.  **Improper Integrals:** Describes Type I (infinite intervals) and Type II (unbounded functions) improper integrals, discusses how to handle multiple singularities, and presents convergence tests for non-negative integrands (Comparison Test, Limit Comparison Test, p-test), as well as concepts of absolute and conditional convergence (including Dirichlet's Test and Cauchy Criterion).
